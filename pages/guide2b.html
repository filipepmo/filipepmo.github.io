Software Delivery Performance

Shorten the Feedback Loop

If you can focus on one thing only it is to shorten the feedback loop.

Why? Because of the nature of software development being a creative endeavour, iterative and experimental instead of following the same steps to produce the same outcome (repetitive tasks). In other words, agile. You want to see the results of your code by integrating many times and often (CI/CD).

The first consequence is that inside the team you should push out stuff very quickly. Less than a day is the goal.

The second consequence is that you should push often.

Using DORA Metrics

Gene Kim, Jez Humble and Nicolas Forsgren in their book Accelerate: Building and Scaling High Performing Technology Organizations have compiled the probably most comprehensive analysis of our craft.

It is important that this metrics are used to guide the team in finding the best engineering practices and not interpreted as targets that need to be hit. Making the metrics targets will only make people try to game the system and lose their self-introspective value.

Through six years of research, the DevOps Research and Assessment (DORA) team has identified four key metrics that indicate the performance of a software development team:

  • Deployment Frequency
    • How often an organization successfully releases to production
  • Lead Time for Changes
    • The amount of time it takes a commit to get into production
  • Change Failure Rate
    • The percentage of deployments causing a failure in production
  • Time to Restore Service
    • How long it takes an organization to recover from a failure in production

At a high level, Deployment Frequency and Lead Time for Changes measure velocity, while Change Failure Rate and Time to Restore Service measure stability.
