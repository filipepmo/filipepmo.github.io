Risks and Mitigations

# Risk Probability Impact Mitigation
1 Spreads itself too thin by taking responsibility for too many services at once. High Constantly firefighting doesn’t have time to address risk in a more permanent way Engages initially on a single important service
2 Becomes too introspective trying to understand SRE principles and how to implement them. Medium Underdelivers. For e.g. the team might become consumed with developing the perfect SLO definition, neglecting the needs of the service in the meantime. Partners with the current Operational team and works on features specific to reliability and integration with existing operational platforms.
3 Abandons SRE principles and practices in order to meet product milestones. High Reliability improvements to defend the SLO, such as architectural changes, may never be implemented because they set back development timelines. Has input into the design, with a particular focus on defining SLOs and analyzing reliability risks inherent in the design
4 Gets distracted by conflict with existing teams that perceive a loss of influence or power as a result of the new responsibilities. Depends Medium Manages communication of the change very carefully.
5 Does not have the necessary breadth of skills, so delivers only part of the necessary improvements. Low Without the ability to, for example, to use PromQL, may be unable to instrument the product to measure reliability correctly. Is not expected to have operational responsibility on day one;
6 Sees no benefit to their day-to-day routine after a few months. Medium Doesn’t support the change to have more operational responsibilities. Regularly reviews progress internally and with stakeholders.
7 Has no slack capacity they can leverage to change the team’s day-to-day activities. Depends Doesn’t support the change to have more operational responsibilities. Secures senior leadership support for the change. Renegotiates responsibilities to create the slack needed to effect change.
8 Doesn’t consistently have the skills needed to evolve toward SRE, or an interest in acquiring the skills. High Doesn’t support the change to have more operational responsibilities. Has access to robust personal and technical support throughout the transition. Adds an experienced SRE or developer to the team.
9 Perceives that the conversion process is the start of a slow journey to job losses as automation replaces humans. Depends Doesn’t support the change to have more operational responsibilities. Deals with the concern about job losses head on. In a lot of environments, automation eliminates portions of work, but not jobs as a whole; while this might be a step on the path to job losses, it does at least have the virtue of freeing up time to do something better (and more sellable to a future employer) than nonautomated toil.
